    02 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

    Executive Summary

    A structured, volume-tiered dealer incentive program built around Bosch's two core circular saw blade sizes.

    Objective

    Grow sell-through of Bosch Eco for Wood circular saw blades (184mm/7" & 235mm/9") across CME's UAE trade network, using a volume-tiered voucher plus a sampling and point-of-sale layer.

    Campaign Duration

    1 July – 31 December 2026 (6 months), structured in four execution phases — see Execution Timeline (10).

    Business Purpose

    Defend and grow Bosch's counter presence in a category where Milwaukee, DeWalt, Makita and Hilti compete directly, while closing out the sub-dealer network to a confirmed 100-counter footprint.

    Expected Outcomes

    Full activation of 50 POS units and 1,500 free samples; a combined ~1.7× H1→H2 volume ask across the 5 anchor accounts, governed by an AED 29,500 voucher ceiling.

    NoteOutcome-level KPIs — revenue uplift, ROI, market share — are not defined in the source data. See KPIs (16) for what is confirmed today.

    03 — CAMPAIGN VISION

    Campaign Vision

    Why this campaign, and why now.

    Why

    Consumables — blades and discs — are where a trade counter's repeat-order habit gets set. Whoever owns the accessory relationship at the counter tends to keep the power-tool relationship too.

    Business Need

    Milwaukee, DeWalt, Makita and Hilti compete for the same counter shelf space. A volume-tiered voucher aimed directly at the counter is the standard trade lever to defend and grow it.

    Dealer Value

    Margin uplift through uncapped, repeatable 50-pc tier vouchers; free-of-cost samples to trial with customers; a physical POS unit for shelf visibility.

    Customer Value

    Better availability of genuine Bosch stock at trusted local counters, and a hands-on sample to try before committing to a bulk purchase.

    04 — ABOUT CME UAE

    About CME UAE

    Company overview, from CME's own public profile.

    Central Motors & Equipment LLC

    CME is a member of ALFAHIM Automotive, one of the larger family business groups in the UAE. The company holds exclusive UAE distribution rights for a set of internationally known brands — including Bosch and Dremel in power tools, and Michelin, Osram and Valeo in the automotive space — operating across four divisions: Tyres, Automotive Parts, Service, and Power Tools.

    Distribution Footprint

    Retail and wholesale presence in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Al Ain and Sharjah.

    Power Tools Mandate

    CME is the sole UAE distributor for Bosch and Dremel, spanning professional power tools, DIY, hobby tools and accessories — the exact category this campaign sits in.

    Background

    Publicly listed as founded in 1976, headquartered in Sharjah, UAE.

    07 — COUNTERTOP DISPLAY STRATEGY

    Countertop Display

    The physical touchpoint of the campaign, at the counter itself.

    Illustrative mock-up — countertop POS unit with sample blade window and card holder.

    Placement Strategy

    Installed at all confirmed sub-dealer counters, phased by territory — priority to the 89 confirmed counters in July, extending to the remaining 11 as the list closes.

    Sits at eye level near the checkout, holding physical 7" and 9" samples plus take-away cards, prompting a hands-on comparison before purchase.

    Benefits

    Passive 24/7 visibility, a natural staff-led upsell trigger, and a consistent Bosch presence across every counter regardless of its size.

    10 — EXECUTION TIMELINE

    Execution Timeline

    Four phases, July through December, each with one clear objective.

    01
    July

    Launch & Setup

    • Close the gap — confirm the last 10 Dubai + 1 Sharjah/NE counters.
    • Confirm dealer-of-record for the 25 flagged counters against the 5 tracked accounts (Fine Tools, Tyche, Fakhruddin Kader Bhai, Saeed Al Zaabi, Al Majarrah).
    • Install 50 POS units at confirmed counters (20 DXB / 15 AUH / 15 SHJ-NE).
    • Start sample distribution — 5+5 pcs per confirmed counter.
    • Brief all accounts on the tiered voucher rule and invoice-tracking process.
    02
    Aug – Sep

    Activation

    • Visit Fine Tools & Al Majarrah to convert their high growth-ask into real orders.
    • First redemption cycle — vouchers issued against dealer invoices.
    • Push the direct/industry target — 300 pcs across all three territories.
    • Track monthly against Est. Voucher, not the flat monthly-pace figure.
    03
    Oct – Nov

    Push & Correction

    • Mid-program review (early Oct) — actual redemption vs. AED 29,500 budget, by account and SKU.
    • Reallocate field support if the two high-multiplier accounts are behind.
    • Restock sample cards where the initial 5+5 has run out.
    04
    December

    Close-out

    • Final redemption window — last vouchers processed against invoice.
    • Reconcile spend against the AED 29,500 budget.
    • Capture sell-through data for the H1 2027 baseline.
